## Problem Summary
`pe-622` asks for the sum of all even deck sizes `n` such that a perfect out-shuffle (top and bottom fixed) has period `s(n)=60`, modulo `99991`.

## Correct Answer
`43260`

## Correct Approach
1. For even `n`, index positions `0..n-1` from top to bottom. The out-shuffle map is:
   - `i -> 2i` for `i < n/2`
   - `i -> 2(i-n/2)+1` for `i >= n/2`
   which is equivalent to `i -> 2i (mod n-1)` on `0..n-2`, with bottom card fixed.
2. Therefore:
   `s(n) = ord_{n-1}(2)`.
3. Let `m = n-1` (odd). Need `ord_m(2)=60`.
   So `m | (2^60 - 1)` and order must be exact (not a proper divisor of 60).
4. Factor:
   `2^60 - 1 = 3^2 * 5^2 * 7 * 11 * 13 * 31 * 41 * 61 * 151 * 331 * 1321`.
5. Use CRT:
   `ord_m(2) = lcm(ord_{p_i^{e_i}}(2))`.
   Prime-power orders used here:
   - `ord_3(2)=2`, `ord_9(2)=6`
   - `ord_5(2)=4`, `ord_25(2)=20`
   - `ord_7(2)=3`, `ord_11(2)=10`, `ord_13(2)=12`, `ord_31(2)=5`, `ord_41(2)=20`, `ord_61(2)=60`, `ord_151(2)=15`, `ord_331(2)=30`, `ord_1321(2)=60`
6. Enumerate all `4608` divisors `m` of `2^60-1`, keep those with `ord_m(2)=60` (`4456` values), and sum `n=m+1`.
7. The total sum modulo `99991` is `43260`.

## Wrong Approaches
### Wrong Branch A: In-shuffle indexing (`ord_{n+1}(2)` instead of `ord_{n-1}(2)`)
- Uses the wrong permutation model (`n+1` modulus), which corresponds to a different shuffle convention.
- Produces an incorrect modulo result: `34348`.

### Wrong Branch B: Using `ord_m(2) | 60` instead of `ord_m(2)=60`
- Sums all `m | (2^60-1)` without enforcing exact order.
- This overcounts many decks with smaller periods and gives wrong modulo `62239`.

### Wrong Branch C: Requiring a prime factor with order exactly 60
- Forces inclusion of `61` or `1321`, missing valid composites whose order reaches `60` via LCM of smaller orders.
- This undercounts and gives wrong modulo `92518`.
